Team 2
Before the Moment Passes

Reese has been building toward this moment for two weeks. She's leading the team meeting to introduce a new AI workflow tool — part of a broader initiative rolling out across the team. She's done the work.

She shares her screen. Walks them through it. Then she looks at Dev's camera: "Okay so — this is going to sound like a lot but it's actually really straightforward once you're in it. And Dev, honestly — you know this work better than anyone on this team. You'd be so good at this."

Dev is looking at the tool on his screen, not at Reese. "How long did you actually test this for? Because I — " He pauses. "I spent a whole afternoon on something similar once. Looked totally fine. Sent it up. It wasn't."

Reese responds, "No I know, I know — but that's why I've been in this for weeks, I didn't just — this isn't me just throwing something at the wall, you know? I mean — it works. I've seen it work."

Carmen, not quite looking at her camera either: "No yeah, I get that. I just — every time we add one of these things something kind of... quietly goes away? And then we look up and we don't know how to do it without the tool anymore. You know?" She clicks something offscreen. "Also — and this might be a whole other thing — do we know what the actual cost of running these is? I don't know. Maybe that's not for today."

Nobody picks it up.

Dev still hasn't looked at his camera. Reese has stopped sharing her screen without saying so.

The Question Nobody Answered

Some context before today's meeting:

Last week Theo slacked you — they just got a BTS Claude license and have been loving it. You could feel the excitement even through Slack.

Nadia slacked you separately around the same time. She has ChatGPT access but has barely touched it. "I just haven't had time to figure it out honestly." You know how stretched she's been.

Today's meeting:

The project: a kickoff deck that's been pushed three times and still isn't done.

Nadia → owns the deck end-to-end

Marcus → waiting on Nadia's deck to complete his task

Theo → waiting on Marcus's edits to finish their part

Theo speaks up first. "I just got access to Claude — I could actually pull from some of our strongest past kickoff decks and build something from those. Might be faster than starting from scratch."

Marcus: "Yeah I mean — whatever gets this done. I've been sitting on my piece for a while now. The tool I have access to can't really get this to client-ready on its own." He doesn't say how long he's been waiting. He doesn't have to.

Theo adds: "But before we do anything — can we make sure we actually understand why this keeps stalling? I don't want to put a tech solution on top of a people problem."

Nadia doesn't respond. Marcus doesn't respond.

Nobody answers the question.
